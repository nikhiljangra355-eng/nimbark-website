Is taking research support services legal and ethical?

Yes. Professional academic consultancy is a legal and well-established service in India. We work as research consultants — guiding and supporting you with research design, analysis, editing, formatting and publication, much like a statistician, language editor or private tutor would. The research remains yours: we encourage scholars to use our support in line with their university's policies, and PhD admission, evaluation, thesis approval and the award of degrees always remain the sole prerogative of the respective university.

What is your revision policy?

Feedback from your supervisor or research committee is a normal and expected part of the research process. Therefore, revisions are built into every project.

Before a project begins, the scope of work and the number of revision rounds are mutually agreed upon during the initial discussion. Nimbark Research Insights will provide revisions in accordance with that agreement, at no additional cost, provided the requested changes fall within the agreed project scope.

Any revisions arising from your supervisor's or committee's comments on the delivered work will be completed free of charge up to the agreed number of revision rounds and within the defined scope of the project.

However, if the project scope changes — for example, if your supervisor requests a new chapter, a different research design, revised research objectives, additional analysis, or any work beyond the originally agreed scope — we will provide a transparent quotation for the additional work before proceeding. Such work will only commence after your approval.

Our goal is to provide a transparent, professional, and client-focused revision process while ensuring the highest standards of quality and timely delivery.

What is the difference between UGC-CARE and Scopus journals? Which should I choose?

UGC-CARE is the University Grants Commission's list of approved journals, generally sufficient for PhD course-work requirements and most Indian university norms. Scopus is a global citation database of higher-impact international journals — usually needed for academic recruitment, promotions and international recognition. Scopus publication takes longer and review is stricter. We assess your purpose, field and deadline and recommend the right target — read our detailed guide: UGC-CARE vs Scopus.
