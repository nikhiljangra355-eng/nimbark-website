Frequently Asked Questions

The questions scholars ask us most often — answered honestly. Can't find yours? Contact us any time.

Is my work and identity kept 100% confidential?

Yes, absolutely. Confidentiality is the foundation of our service. Your name, university, research topic, data and documents are accessible only to the small team assigned to your project, and are never shared, resold or reused. We are happy to sign a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) before you share anything with us, and we delete project files on request after completion.

Do you provide a Turnitin plagiarism report with the work?

Yes. Every written deliverable — synopsis, chapters, papers or the full thesis — is checked through Turnitin before delivery, and the similarity report is shared with you along with the final files. If your university uses a specific similarity threshold (commonly 10% under UGC norms), we work to bring the document safely below it.

Is the writing done by humans? Will it pass AI-detection tools?

Your work is researched and written by qualified subject-matter experts — real academics from your discipline. Because it is genuine human scholarship built on actual sources, your own data and your inputs, it reads and tests as human writing. We do not paste machine-generated text into academic documents, and we stand behind every deliverable through your university's checks.

What is your revision policy?

Feedback is a normal part of research, so revisions are built into every project. Changes arising from your supervisor's or committee's comments on delivered work are unlimited and free within the project's agreed scope. If the scope itself changes — for example, your guide asks for a new chapter or a different research design — we quote the addition transparently before doing anything.

How long will my project take?

Typical timelines: a synopsis takes 7–15 days; data analysis 1–3 weeks depending on the dataset and techniques; a complete PhD thesis 2–4 months delivered chapter by chapter; plagiarism removal 3–10 days; and journal publication depends on the journal's review cycle (typically 2–6 months for UGC-CARE and longer for Scopus/SCI). We commit to a written timeline before starting and deliver in stages so you can track progress.

Can I pay in installments?

Yes. Every larger project is split into clear milestones — for example: synopsis → chapters 1–2 → data analysis → remaining chapters → final formatting. You pay per milestone, after seeing the delivered work for that stage. There is no requirement to pay the full amount upfront.

Which subjects and disciplines do you cover?

When you enquire, we confirm honestly whether we have the right expert for your specific topic before accepting the project.

What is the difference between UGC-CARE and Scopus journals? Which should I choose?

UGC-CARE is the University Grants Commission's list of approved journals, generally sufficient for PhD course-work requirements and most Indian university norms. Scopus is a global citation database of higher-impact international journals — usually needed for academic recruitment, promotions and international recognition. Scopus publication takes longer and review is stricter. We assess your purpose, field and deadline and recommend the right target — read our detailed guide: UGC-CARE vs Scopus.
