When the Similarity Report Threatens Your Submission

Under the UGC's plagiarism regulations, most Indian universities now require a thesis similarity index below 10% before acceptance, with escalating penalties above it. A high similarity score close to your submission date is one of the most stressful situations in a research career — and one of the most misunderstood. High similarity usually doesn't mean dishonesty; it means standard definitions, review-of-literature passages and methodology descriptions were kept too close to their sources.

Our subject experts fix this the only legitimate way: by genuinely rewriting the flagged passages — re-expressing each idea in fresh academic language, restructuring sentences and paragraphs, and ensuring every source remains properly cited. We never use white-text tricks, character substitution, quote-abuse or machine spinners. Those shortcuts are detected by modern detection algorithms and can convert a similarity problem into a misconduct case. Real paraphrasing by someone who understands your subject is the only method that is safe, permanent and exam-committee-proof.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the meaning of my thesis change?

No. Paraphrasing is done by an expert from your discipline whose brief is to preserve your argument exactly while changing its expression. You receive a tracked-changes version so you can verify every edit — and anything you want re-done differently is revised free.

Is the plagiarism report you provide genuine?

Yes — we scan through licensed professional plagiarism software and give you the complete original PDF report (similarity view with source breakdown), which your university can cross-verify. The scan runs in no-repository mode so your document is not stored in any database before your official submission.

Can you also fix AI-generated content flags?

Yes. If earlier drafting help has left machine-flavoured text in your document, our experts rewrite those sections as genuine human academic prose. Combined with the similarity work, this leaves your document safe on both fronts — because it genuinely is human-written.
