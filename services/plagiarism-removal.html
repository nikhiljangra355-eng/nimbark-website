When the Similarity Report Threatens Your Submission

Under the UGC's plagiarism regulations, most Indian universities now require a thesis similarity index below 10% before acceptance, with escalating penalties above it. A high Turnitin score close to your submission date is one of the most stressful situations in a research career — and one of the most misunderstood. High similarity usually doesn't mean dishonesty; it means standard definitions, review-of-literature passages and methodology descriptions were kept too close to their sources.

Our subject experts fix this the only legitimate way: by genuinely rewriting the flagged passages — re-expressing each idea in fresh academic language, restructuring sentences and paragraphs, and ensuring every source remains properly cited. We never use white-text tricks, character substitution, quote-abuse or machine spinners. Those shortcuts are detected by modern Turnitin algorithms and can convert a similarity problem into a misconduct case. Real paraphrasing by someone who understands your subject is the only method that is safe, permanent and exam-committee-proof.

Frequently Asked Questions

No. Paraphrasing is done by an expert from your discipline whose brief is to preserve your argument exactly while changing its expression. You receive a tracked-changes version so you can verify every edit — and anything you want re-done differently is revised free.

Is the Turnitin report you provide genuine?

Yes — we scan through a licensed Turnitin account and give you the complete original PDF report (similarity view with source breakdown), which your university can cross-verify. We use the no-repository mode so your document is not stored in Turnitin's database before your official submission.

Yes. If earlier drafting help has left machine-flavoured text in your document, our experts rewrite those sections as genuine human academic prose. Combined with the similarity work, this leaves your document safe on both fronts — because it genuinely is human-written.
